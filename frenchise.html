These Are the Highest-Grossing Movie Franchises of All Time

These are the 10 highest-grossing movie franchises of all time.

BY Timea Betáková

The film industry is a constant flow of changing numbers and releases that we, as film lovers, have to keep an eye on. Although the audience might not be as interested in the grossing numbers as the franchises themselves, it’s always mind-blowing to see just how many people around the globe pay for the same cinema ticket as you do. More than that, box office numbers indicate to studios which movies do well and which don't, influencing executive decisions about future projects they might invest in.

With the release of Spider-man: No Way Home, which overtook Avatar at the domestic box office, and this year's premiere of The Batman starring Robert Pattinson, the statistics for highest-grossing franchise will probably keep changing. Take a look at the 10 highest-grossing movie franchises of all time as of July 2022.

10th The Lord of the Rings — $5.8B

There's been heavy discourse about the upcoming Amazon Prime TV series based on J.R.Tolkien's Lord of the Rings books called Lord of the Rings: The Rings Of Power. The disappointment appears to be universal: from the costumes, to dwarves without beards and to them getting many details wrong. It probably won't gain too much of an audience like the original trilogy had managed. We're talking about a trilogy that was directed by Peter Jackson and had all three films shot simultaneously, starring big names such as Elijah Wood, Ian McKellen, Cate Blanchett, and Orlando Bloom. Jackson has definitely set the bars high for fantasy franchises, given trilogy has grossed over $5.8B.

9th X-Men — $6.07B

Stan Lee and Jack Kirby are back at it again with rightfully taking credit for creating the X-Men mutants and allowing it to become the eighth highest-grossing movie franchise of all time with $6.06B. Since the Marvel Studios obtained rights to the X-Men franchise, it was only a matter of time until the worlds would merge together on screen. With the recent release of Doctor Strange: Multiverse of Madness, we can certainly confirm that the X-Men will appear in the MCU franchise. Although there have already been some fandom disputes, we're excited to see how this works out.

8th Fast and Furious — $6.6B+

Looking back at the Fast and Furious franchise, it could get a little ridiculous and unrealistic — and yet, you can't help but completely love everything about it. The franchise has grossed over $6.6B throughout 20 years so far. We still have two more movies to look forward to, which will conclude the series. The franchise perfectly mixes the sense of family with danger and illegal wrongdoings. We can also thank it for thrusting Vin Diesel and Paul Walker to stardom.

7th Batman — $6.8B

With the release of The Batman starring Robert Pattinson and Zoe Kravitz earlier this year, the Batman franchise has moved up a few spots. The DC Comics hero has been adapted more than 13 times over the span of 82 years, and the craze probably won't die down anytime soon. Indeed, $6.8 billion is more than enough to confirm that the fandom wants to see more of Gotham City's Caped Crusader.

6th The Avengers — $7.76B

We all know just how massive the MCU is, but you still have to admit that four films alone grossing over $7.76B is more than impressive. Has anyone ever watched the Avengers quadrilogy independently of the other movies? Does it still make sense? Sometimes it feels nostalgic to think about what it was like back in 2012 when everyone had their floor in the Avenger's Tower. But we have to accept that the chapter is already closed — for now. With new MCU heroes joining the fray, new Avengers team-ups and formations are bound to happen.

5th James Bond — $7.88B

Sean Connery, David Niven, George Lazenby, Roger Moore, Timothy Dalton, Pierce Brosnan, and Daniel Craig have all portrayed the agent 007 by the name of James Bond from 1962 to 2021. This equals to 27 movies that have grossed over $7.88B worldwide. Despite some criticisms of the franchise's narrative elements, it's no secret that Bond is the most renowned spy character in the history of cinema. The amount of speculation that has gone into the search for the next James Bond is a testament to the franchise's longevity.

4th Harry Potter — $9.20B

The success of the Harry Potter book series was a big indicator of how the films would do when Warner Bros. decided to develop them. Ten movies have managed to gross over $9.20B in the worldwide box office. All four directors that worked on the movies (Chris Columbus, Alfonso Cuarón, Mike Newell, and David Yates) put their unique spin on the style and the storytelling. The Fantastic Beasts is a great spin-off prequel, but we would also definitely love to see a series dedicated to the Marauders' era at Hogwarts.

3rd Spider-Man — $9.25B+

It's only recently that the Spider-Man franchise took Harry Potter's third place as the highest grossing franchise. It's all thanks to the release of the latest Spider-Man: No Way Home movie, which has grossed over $1.53 billion worldwide. Although Kevin Feige confirmed that they are developing a fourth MCU Spider-Man movie, it's hard to say how it's going to work out given the ongoing conflict between Disney and Sony. No matter the drama and disputes, Spider-Man still ranks third with over $9.25B grossed.

2nd Star Wars — $10.31B+

Nine movies grossing over $10.31B and all nominated for the Academy Awards at some point — this is as clear an indication of both critical and commercial success as can be. We wonder if it was George Lucas' intention to make a pop-cultural phenomenon out of a sci-fi movie, something never seen before the 70s. With 3 separate film trilogies and another planned trilogy on the way, it's safe to say that Star Wars will more than likely keep its spot on this list.

1st Marvel Cinematic Universe — $26.6B+

At last, we have a franchise that is so far ahead with the box office numbers, that it's nearly impossible to take its place. It certainly helps there are over two dozen movies in the franchise so far, but it doesn't mean each of those movies aren't indeed commercial successes. From Iron Man in 2008 to, most recently, Doctor Strange in the Multiverse of Madness in May 2022, the Marvel Cinematic Universe keeps proving there are more stories to tell. Even though some are afraid the universe is getting too complicated and nonsensical, the $26.6 billion mark is a sign they won't stop anytime soon.
