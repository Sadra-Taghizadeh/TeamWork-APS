┨ ┩ ┪ ┫Psycho (1960)┠ ┡ ┢ ┣
The American slasher genre would not exist without Psycho, Alfred Hitchcock's film about a man's (Anthony Perkins) horrifying obsession with his mother and
the Freudian perils of parents prohibiting their children from being autonomous adults for fear of having empty nest syndrome. The film is infamous for its
surprising nihilism, its decision to quit following the main star halfway through the film, and its disturbing sense of unease which was a catalyst for a whole new
wave of atmospheric horror.
┨ ┩ ┪ ┫Get Out (2017)┠ ┡ ┢ ┣
Get out is a horror movie that tackles racism like no horror movie ever has before. It is a layered film, and each layer has something to say about stereotypes,
racism, and white supremacy.
┨ ┩ ┪ ┫A Quiet Place (2018)┠ ┡ ┢ ┣
A family struggles for survival in a world where most humans have been killed by blind but noise-sensitive creatures. They are forced to communicate
in sign language to keep the creatures at bay.
┨ ┩ ┪ ┫The Texas Chainsaw Massacre (1974)┠ ┡ ┢ ┣
Tobe Hooper's The Texas Chainsaw Massacre is an exploration of the bizarre macabre, using the predatorial murders of a group of '70s kids
as a launching point for something truly important and terrifying. Taking after the themes of Wes Craven's The Last House on the Left in 1972,
the film explores the most extreme version of American freedom and the essence of cult, released five years after the Manson murders in 1969
and one year before the end of the Vietnam War in 1975.
┨ ┩ ┪ ┫US (2019)┠ ┡ ┢ ┣
Adelaide Wilson and her family are attacked by mysterious figures dressed in red. Upon closer inspection, the Wilsons realise that the intruders are
exact lookalikes of them.
